Value

Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

March 1st, 2015

Summary

Last week I learned about Stereotype Threat. One of the antidotes identified by Claude M. Steele in his book is affirmation. In this blog I identify values that are important to me and consider how my values may help combat Stereotype Threat at Dev Bootcamp.

When you think of the times in your life where you’ve been the happiest, the proudest, or the most satisfied, which of the following values come to mind?

Accompshment 5 ** Close relationships 3 ** Economic security 4 ** Excitement 3 ** Meaningful work 5 ** Faith 5 ** Spirituaty 5 ** Helping others 5 ** Adventure 3 ** Autonomy 4 ** Growth 5 ** Honesty 5 **

Accomplishment

This value is important to me for various reasons. I think I owe it to my family to succeed and accomplish great things because they sacrificed a lot and worked really hard to give me the opportunities and life I have today. I also think that accomplishments in life lead to growth and success. I don’t remember a single time in my life where I wasn’t working to accomplish something.

What was the last topic that someone asked for your advice on? Did it relate to any of the values you found important?

Yes. I often help my friends and family with school applications, programs, and scholarships. I guess that ties into accomplishment, growth, and meaningful work. I’ve been able to do a lot and receive aid when I needed it. This makes me an “expert” on the topic according to them, and I always help anyway I can. I do a lot of mentoring as a consequence of this.

How do you feel when you think about your values? Do you think it can help you mediate stereotype threat if you recognize you feel it?

I’m not certain that my values can mediate stereotype threat, I’m not sure how to use them in that sense. However, I understand my values very clearly and they ground me whenever I start to question or doubt myself.

Can you think of any other exercises you can use to help you when you are feeling down (for whatever reason) at DBC?

Remembering past accomplishments is usually a big motivator for me, it reminds me that if I did it before I can do it again. I also carry a picture of my parents in my wallet that I look at and it reminds me of my purpose, of my ultimate mission. It doesn’t allow me to give up. When I am down I usually call my fiancé and he encourages me to never compare myself to others, and to keep pushing because no one was born knowing, everyone had to learn and I can too. Prayer is also something I do often and daily. My spiritual life and faith keep me strong in hardest of times, it gives me supernatural hope. I think these are all things I can do and are very practical in nature.
