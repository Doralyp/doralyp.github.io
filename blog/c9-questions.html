Asking Good Questions

How to ask the right question to get the right answer

March 11th, 2015

Have you ever posted on the Google+ community, or Stack Overflow and didn't get the answer you needed? Were you frustrated by not receiving a helpful response? Did you review the question and consider why people weren't answering? Did you not give enough information? Was the question asked elsewhere? Was it unclear? Learning how to ask good questions is a necessary skill for success as a developer and a student. So let’s go over some tips on how to ask good questions.

Tips:

  • Make an interesting title, keep it simple, specific, and get to the point. No one has time to figure out what you are trying to say, they are already giving their time trying to help, so don’t make it harder than it should be.
  • Summarize the problem, don’t go over a sentence in length. Be precise and informative.
  • Research and share what you have found so far. This shows that you aren’t duplicating questions or simply being lazy.
  • Share unusual circumstances that make your question different from similar questions already online.
  • Include just enough code to allow others to reproduce the problem.
  • Include all the relevant tags, this makes searching and getting responses quicker and more accurate.
  • Proof-read before posting. Make sure the title matches the question. Use capital letters when appropriate for easier reading and always look for typos.
  • Ask and respond to feedback. Say thank you to all who respond, everyone appreciates a little courtesy.

Examples: 

      Wrong:
      HELP! Video doesn't work properly on my laptop!
    
      OK:
      X.org 6.8.1 misshapen mouse cursor, Fooware MV1005 vid. chipset
    
      Best:
      X.org 6.8.1 mouse cursor on Fooware MV1005 vid. chipset - is misshapen

So now go out there and ask those awesome questions. If you seek, you shall find, right? No one ever won the battles they never fought, so give it a try. As usual comment below, send me your thoughts, ideas, feedback, etc. Love to hear from you!
